From the U.S.

Remember - you need a passport! Make sure yours is valid, and don't wait to renew.

From New England: Aer Lingus is the least expensive airline to offer multiple daily direct flights from Logan Airport to Dublin. In some cases, you can get even better fares flying Norwegian Air out of Providence.

From Philadelphia: Aer Lingus is the least expensive airline flying direct from Philadelphia to Dublin, though there is only one daily option. Finnair is a reasonably priced alternative.


From Europe

Getting to Dublin: Aer Lingus and Ryanair cover most destinations in Europe. Ryanair tends to be the cheapest.

Getting Around Ireland

Car Rental: The wedding venue, Tulfarris, is outside of the city, so you will likely want to rent a car if you are coming from abroad. You can rent a car at Dublin Airport, which offers many of the same rental companies as those found in the U.S. (Avis, Budget, Enterprise, and Hertz) as well as several European companies.
Note that in Ireland, there are stricter drunk driving laws, and you drive on the left side of the road (opposite the U.S.).

Taxis and Public Transit: Uber and Lyft aren't as mainstream as in the U.S. MyTaxi is an option for hailing taxis from your phone. There is also public transit via busses, trains, and trolleys for travel in and arround Dublin.

Getting Around Europe

Once across the Atlantic, it's very easy to get around Europe. We have gotten flights for as low as 20 Euro! Aer Lingus and Ryanair cover most destinations in Europe. Ryanair tends to be the cheapest.
